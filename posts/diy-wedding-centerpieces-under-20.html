Why DIY Your Centerpieces?

DIY centerpieces give you complete control over the design, allow you to match your exact color scheme, and save hundreds (or even thousands) of dollars compared to professional florist arrangements. Plus, you can make them ahead of time and store them until your big day.

10 Budget-Friendly Centerpiece Ideas

1. Mason Jar Floral Arrangements

Classic and versatile, mason jars work for any wedding style. Fill them with seasonal flowers from your local farmer's market or grocery store.

  • Supplies: Mason jars ($1-2 each), fresh flowers ($8-12), twine or ribbon ($2-3)
  • Total Cost: $11-17 per centerpiece

2. Candlelit Lantern Centerpieces

Create a romantic atmosphere with simple lanterns and candles. Add greenery or small flowers around the base for extra elegance.

  • Supplies: Lanterns ($5-8 each), pillar candles ($2-3), greenery ($3-5)
  • Total Cost: $10-16 per centerpiece

3. Wine Bottle Vases

Upcycle empty wine bottles into elegant vases. Remove labels, paint them in your wedding colors, or leave them clear for a minimalist look.

  • Supplies: Wine bottles (free), paint (optional, $3-5), flowers ($5-8)
  • Total Cost: $5-13 per centerpiece

4. Succulent Terrariums

Succulents are affordable, low-maintenance, and make beautiful centerpieces. These can double as wedding favors!

  • Supplies: Glass containers ($3-5 each), succulents ($2-3 each), decorative sand/rocks ($2-3)
  • Total Cost: $7-11 per centerpiece

5. Wooden Crate Arrangements

Small wooden crates make perfect rustic centerpieces. Fill them with a mix of flowers, greenery, and candles.

  • Supplies: Wooden crates ($4-6 each), flowers and greenery ($8-10), tea lights ($2-3)
  • Total Cost: $14-19 per centerpiece

Tips for Success

  • Start early and practice making a few before the wedding
  • Buy flowers the day before or morning of the wedding
  • Keep arrangements simple â€” less is often more
  • Consider the height â€” you want guests to see each other across the table
  • Have backup supplies in case something doesn't work out

With these budget-friendly ideas, you can create stunning centerpieces that reflect your personal style without breaking the bank. Happy crafting!
